## TL;DR
Dedicated review of diffusion-model-based image data augmentation, organizing methods into a taxonomy and surveying the models, evaluation metrics, and open challenges of the field.

## Method
Builds a taxonomy along three dimensions: (1) semantic manipulation techniques, (2) personalization and adaptation strategies, and (3) application-specific augmentation tasks. Reviews the fundamental principles, architectures, and training strategies of diffusion models, plus performance-assessment methodologies and their evaluation metrics. Emphasizes editing/modifying a given image in a "context- and semantics-aware way" (abstract, arXiv:2407.04103).

## Main claim + result
Positions diffusion-based augmentation as a distinct, fast-maturing sub-field that earlier surveys (focused on classical augmentation or general diffusion models) under-cover. The abstract reports no single benchmark number; it is a methods/metrics review — per-method results sit in the cited primary works.

## Relevance to us
Directly maps stage 3 (synthesis) of the loop: it is the menu of diffusion augmentation techniques we can draw from, and its evaluation-metric survey tells us how the field measures augmentation quality — useful when we need to judge whether our generated elevator/e-bike images actually help.
